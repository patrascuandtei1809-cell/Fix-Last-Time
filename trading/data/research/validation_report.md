## 🟢 ROBUST — EMA_MACD_RSI_VOLUME_V2 @ 4h ETH

- **Longer history:** +0.837%/trade over **295 trades / 8.8 years**, PF 1.40,
  Sharpe 2.43, total return +616%.
- **Walk-forward:** **5/5 folds positive** (incl. folds spanning the 2018 and
  2022 bear markets). Weakest fold still break-even-positive (+0.008%).
- **Monte Carlo:** P(expectancy>0) = **99.3%**; bootstrap 90% CI =
  [+0.278%, +1.389%] — **lower bound still positive**; bootstrap PF 5th pct 1.12.
- **Sensitivity:** positive under EVERY scenario — fee ±50%, slip ±50%, ATR
  SL ±25%, ATR TP ±25%. Worst case (ATR-TP −25%) still +0.369%/trade, PF 1.19,
  Sharpe 1.36. Not dependent on a narrow parameter set.
- ⚠️ **Drawdown risk is real:** reshuffled max drawdown median −43%, worst-5%
  −60%. The edge is positive but the equity path is volatile.

## 🟡 WEAK — V2 @ 4h SOL

Base is good and it survives fee/slip, but it fails two robustness bars:
- **Narrow-parameter dependence:** under a 25%-tighter stop (ATR-SL −25%) it
  collapses to **+0.018%/trade, Sharpe +0.04** — essentially break-even. A robust
  edge should not hinge on the exact stop distance.
- **Monte Carlo:** P(exp>0) = 89% (< 95%); bootstrap 90% CI lower bound
  **−0.228%** (includes negative); reshuffled worst-5% drawdown −78%.
- Walk-forward 4/5 folds positive (fold 1 slightly negative).

## 🟡 WEAK — Trend Pullback @ 4h SOL

- Survives all fee/slip/parameter perturbations (most stable to params of the three).
- But **Monte Carlo** P(exp>0) = 83%; bootstrap 90% CI lower bound −0.463%
  (includes negative); walk-forward fold 5 negative (−0.360%); worst-5%
  drawdown −78%. The point estimate is positive; the confidence interval is not.

## Bottom line

- **One survivor: V2 @ 4h ETH is genuinely robust** across 8.8 years, walk-forward,
  Monte Carlo, and full fee/slip/parameter sensitivity.
- The two SOL variants are **positive in expectation but not robust** — their
  Monte Carlo confidence intervals dip negative and (for V2 SOL) the edge depends
  on the stop distance.
